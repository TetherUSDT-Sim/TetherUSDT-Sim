Technology Behind TetherUSDT-Simulated

TetherUSDT-Simulated is built on the Binance Smart Chain (BSC), a high-performance blockchain designed for smart contract functionality and low-cost transactions. By leveraging the BEP-20 token standard, the project ensures compatibility with a wide range of DeFi applications, wallets, and blockchain tools.

The smart contract is written in Solidity and compiled using a standard BEP-20 structure to simulate the behavior of a real stablecoin. While it does not hold any real collateral, it mirrors the mechanisms of USDT to allow for realistic testing, research, and academic analysis.

Developers and researchers can interact with the smart contract, verify transactions on BscScan, and use the token within supported wallets like MetaMask and Trust Wallet. The contract is verified and open-source, enabling transparency and further experimentation.

The simulation approach allows for safe testing environments without financial risk, making it ideal for blockchain education, university labs, and early-stage development.
